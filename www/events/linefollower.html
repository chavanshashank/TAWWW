Line Follower

Introduction

The objective of this contest is for a robot to follow a black line on a white background, without losing the line, and navigating several 90 degree turns. The robot to complete the course in the shortest period of time while accurately tracking the course line from start to finish wins.

Rules

  1. DESCRIPTION
    2. In this competition
    • The Robot must follow a line from its beginning to its end.
    • Line is about 2.5 cm black paper on white background.
    • The line following course is a non-crossing line, and may have arcs with different curvatures
    The robot may be
    • An unmodified kit built from a construction set such as LEGO.
    • Built from scratch by the participant.
    • Or built from any combination (a hybrid robot).
    • The robot must be powered by batteries and must not require mains power for anything except charging battery packs.
    • Design: Robot must be a ground wheeled vehicle.
    • Length: Maximum length is 30 cm.
    • Width: Maximum width is 25 cm.
    • Height:Â  No limit.
    • Weight: No limit.
  2. ENROLMENT RULES
    • Any participating robot has to submit itself to the tests.
    • The tests consist in verifying that the dimensions of the robot correspond to the technical specifications.
    • Any robot not satisfying the tests is excluded from the competition.
    • Timekeeper (judge) starts each run with the word “Go”.
    • Time Clock starts when “Go” is called.
    • The clock stops when the end line is reached.
  3. EVALUATION RULES
    • Competition
      1. The robot that finishes the course with the quickest time will be the winner.
      2. The arena of the race has white and black colours.
      3. The Robot must follow the line without any human intervention, and fully autonomous (No remote control or cable).
      4. Time limit for each round will be maximum 7 minutes, the robot with more time than these limit will be disqualified.
      5. A robot cannot use shortcuts, cut corners to complete the course faster. This will be determined by the judge if a robot is using shortcutting tactics.
      6. Losing the Line or stopping any robot that loses the line course or stops in the line in the middle of course must reacquire the line at just before the point where it was lost. In this case a penalty will be pointed and the timer is running.
      7. Flying robots are not permitted due to safety concerns. Walking robots are allowed (although they will obviously be at a disadvantage in some challenges like the straight-line speed test).
      8. In this competition 5 hand touch and 3 skips allowed
    • Evaluation
      1. The winner will be decided based on a point system.
      2. The winner robot is which obtains the maximum point among the participants.
      3. If two teams are for equalities, a new test between both will occur to decide the final winner.
      4. All decisions made by the relevant judge or judges are considered final.

Structure

  1. There will be two challenges (rounds) in the competition and all will be mandatory.
  2. Round 1: The first challenge is to complete the track with hurdles, robots with least time will be qualified for the second challenge
  3. Round 2: In Final challenge, the robot has to complete the given track in certain time limit on the further difficult track as compare to first.
